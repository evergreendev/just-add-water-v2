Urgency

Western South Dakota’s need for supplemental water shifts from a long-term concern to an immediate reality when drought lingers beyond a single season. As streams thin, reservoirs shrink, and demand stays steady, the region quickly reaches a point where existing supplies can’t keep up — making additional water sources essential.

X Won’t this deplete the Missouri River?

Water that flows through western South Dakota returns to the Missouri River because all of western South Dakota is in the Missouri River Basin. The projected amount of water from the Missouri River that would serve all West River is less than one-half of 1% of the Missouri River at its lowest historical flows. As other states plan similar water supply projects, South Dakota should claim its share of the Missouri River. South Dakotans would be short-sighted to not make use of the Missouri, regardless of whether they live on eastern or western sides of the state.

X Can’t we just implement more conservation measures?

Conservation is part of the solution, but western South Dakota cannot conserve its way out of this problem. As our population continues to grow, the existing water supply will not be enough to sustain us. Under drought conditions, we will have even less available water to serve the growing population. Bringing more water to West River will allow future generations to enjoy the way of life we enjoy today. In addition to vocally supporting the need for more water in western South Dakota, there are ways to help our water supply stretch a bit further by adding conservation into your daily life.

  • Water lawns early in the morning and only when soil is dry
  • Install a rain barrel to water gardens
  • Landscape with drought-resistant plants
  • Take shorter showers
  • Turn off water when brushing teeth
  • Check pipes, toilets, and hoses for leaks, fix any that you find
  • Wash full loads in the dishwasher and washing machine
  • Install water efficient dishwashers and washing machines, low-flow showerheads, dual-flush toilets, and smart lawn irrigation systems.

X Is securing supplemental water just a way to attract or support data centers?

No. The effort to bring more water to western South Dakota is based on the area’s vulnerability to drought and its growing population. Western Dakota Regional Water System was formed in 2021 to ensure reliable ongoing access to drinking water for West River communities and rural water systems. If an additional water source isn’t identified and developed soon, we could see sharp increases in water rates and extreme mandatory conservation measures.
